Interpreting Neural Population Dynamics
over time During Neuroplasiticity

Human brain is the inspiration for the advent of artificial intelligence which solves problems that human cannot handle. Still achieving human level intelligence and consciousness in a manmade machine is somewhere in distant future. Perception of the entire surrounding for a human being is just encoded as different patterns of neuronal excitations. Even triggering one of these excitations artificially would create a fake memory which is not even experienced by the brain in its lifetime.
This natural data representation and information processing inside brain enables high level optimization and adaptation to behave with respect to the environment. The goal of this paper is to review the evolution of experiments that proved actions, thoughts and memories are being encoded as different neural excitation patterns and referring current works on internal dynamics of the neural populations while performing activities. Finally arriving at the hypotheses that the way brain encodes information can be interpreted by recording neural population that evolves while learning a new activity. Each updation through time recorded and labelled along with the movements that caused the excitations. Using this data repository to identify the general motifs that brain follows in encoding information and create memories.
